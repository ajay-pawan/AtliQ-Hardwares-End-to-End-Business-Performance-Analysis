# 🟣 AtliQ Hardwares – End-to-End Business Performance Analysis

## 🔷 Objective

To evaluate overall business performance by analyzing **sales growth, customer contribution, product performance, and profitability**, and to identify key drivers, risks, and opportunities for strategic decision-making.

---

# 🟢 1. Sales Analytics

# 🔹 1. Customer Performance

* Total Net Sales reached **883M**, with a strong YoY growth of **204.5%**, increasing from **196.7M in 2020 to 598.9M in 2021**.

* Revenue distribution is **highly concentrated**, where the top 3 customers contribute nearly **34% of total sales**, while the remaining customers contribute **66%**, indicating partial dependency on key accounts.

* This concentration ensures strong revenue generation but also introduces **risk if key customers decline**, highlighting the need for a balanced customer portfolio.

* At the same time, the contribution from a broad set of customers reflects a **diversified customer base**, supporting long-term scalability.

---

### 🟣 Strategic Insight (Customer Channels)

* Company-owned channels such as **AtliQ e Store** and **AtliQ Exclusive** are among the **top-performing contributors** to total revenue.

* As owned distribution channels, they provide **greater control over pricing, customer experience, and margins** compared to third-party customers.

* This creates an opportunity to **expand company-owned stores across high-performing markets**, improving profitability and reducing dependency on external partners.

* Meanwhile, third-party customers should continue to be leveraged for **market expansion and reach**, ensuring a balanced and scalable distribution strategy.

---

# 🔹 2. Market Performance

* Despite strong revenue growth, the business is **not meeting its target expectations**, with an overall shortfall of approximately **-54.9M**.

* Several markets are performing **close to their targets with minimal deviation**, indicating stable performance in select regions.

* However, most markets continue to show a **gap between actual sales and targets**, suggesting challenges in execution or target planning.

* Key markets such as **USA, India, Canada, and the United Kingdom**, although contributing significant revenue, are still **underperforming against targets**.

* This indicates that while demand is increasing globally, there is a need to **optimize market-level strategies and improve alignment between targets and execution**.

---


---

# 🟢 2. Product Analytics

## 🔹 2.1 Product Category Performance

* **Peripherals & Accessories (P&A)** is the dominant revenue segment, contributing **338.4M**, making it the core driver of the business. 

* The **PC segment** shows the highest growth (**~313.7% YoY**), indicating rapidly increasing demand.

* **Networking & Storage (N&S)** provides **stable and consistent revenue contribution**, supporting overall balance.

👉 **Implication:**

* Business is driven by **high-volume accessory products**, while PCs represent a **high-growth opportunity**

---

## 🔹 2.2 New Product Performance (2021)

* Newly launched products generated **176.2M units in 2021**, indicating strong product adoption. 

* P&A products (keyboards and mouse variants such as Qwerty and Trigger series) contributed approximately **~60M units**, forming the largest share.

* PC products (Gen X/Y laptops) contributed around **19.5M units**, reflecting increasing demand in computing devices.

* N&S products (Wi-Fi extenders like Power Dx3) contributed **~17.2M units**, showing stable demand.

👉 **Implication:**

* New product success is largely concentrated in **accessory categories**, with PCs emerging as a **high-growth segment**

---

## 🔹 2.3 Top & Bottom Product Performance

* Top-performing products are primarily from the **P&A segment**, particularly **gaming keyboards and mouse products**, indicating strong demand in these categories. 

* Bottom-performing products show **very low sales volumes**, indicating weak demand, poor positioning, or niche usage.

👉 **Implication:**

* Product portfolio is **skewed**, with a few products driving the majority of volume
* There is a need for **portfolio optimization**

---

## 🔹 2.4 Top Countries Contribution

* The top contributing markets include **India, USA, South Korea, Canada, and the United Kingdom**. 

👉 **Implication:**

* These markets drive a large portion of revenue and therefore have **higher targets**
* Failure to meet these targets reflects **high expectations rather than weak performance**

---

# 🟢 3. Financial Analytics

## 🔹 3.1 Overall Profitability

* Net Sales increased to **598.9M in 2021**, while COGS increased to **380.7M**. 

* Gross Margin % declined from **41.4% (2019) → 37.3% (2020) → 36.4% (2021)**. 

👉 **Key Insight:**

* Revenue growth has been accompanied by **faster cost growth**, leading to declining profitability

👉 **Conclusion:**
**High sales growth has not translated into proportional profit growth**

---

## 🔹 3.2 Country-Level Profitability

* Countries with the highest Gross Margin % include:

  * New Zealand (~48.2%)
  * Japan (~46.5%)
  * United Kingdom (~45.1%)
  * Portugal (~42.1%)
  * Sweden (~40.2%) 

👉 **Implication:**

* High-sales markets are not always the most profitable
* Profitability is driven by **cost efficiency and pricing strategy**

---

## 🔹 3.3 Regional Profitability (Sub-Zone Analysis)

* Sub-zones such as **ANZ, ROA, and SE** consistently show higher Gross Margin % across all years. 

👉 **Implication:**

* Certain regions have **structural advantages** in profitability
* These regions can be used as **benchmark markets for pricing and cost strategies**

---

## 🔹 3.4 Monthly P&L Trends

* Sales show a **strong upward trend** from 2019 to 2021.

* A **sharp decline in March–April 2020** is observed, indicating a potential external disruption affecting operations. 

* Despite recovery and strong growth in 2021, Gross Margin % remains **lower compared to 2019 levels**.

👉 **Implication:**

* Business has scaled rapidly, but **cost management has not improved proportionally**

---

# 🔷 Key Business Insights

* Revenue growth is strong but **not evenly distributed across customers and products**
* The business shows **dependency on a small set of customers**
* Company-owned channels are **strategically important for profitability**
* Markets are growing but **targets are not consistently met**
* Product portfolio is **concentrated in a few high-performing categories**
* Profitability is declining due to **rising cost pressures**
* High revenue does not necessarily indicate **high profitability**

---

# 🔷 Business Recommendations

* Expand **company-owned distribution channels** to improve margin control
* Reduce dependency on top customers by **developing mid-tier customers**
* Focus on scaling **high-growth segments (PCs) and high-volume segments (P&A)**
* Optimize or phase out **low-performing products**
* Reassess **market-level targets** to align with realistic growth potential
* Implement **cost optimization strategies** to improve Gross Margins
* Leverage high-margin regions as **benchmark models for pricing and operations**

---

# 🔷 Final Conclusion

> AtliQ Hardwares has achieved significant revenue growth; however, the analysis highlights key challenges in profitability, customer concentration, and target alignment. Long-term success will depend on improving cost efficiency, optimizing the product portfolio, and strategically expanding high-margin and high-control channels.
